Q1) Why are ventilators provided at the top of the room?

ans: when we exhale hot air, the hot air is lighter and tends to rise to the ceiling
and goes out through the ventilator. As that happens, negative preasure is
created in the room and as there is more preasure outside of the room, cold air
enters the room. So, if the ventiltor is on the top of the room, it works
efficiantly.


Q2) Why is mercury used in clinical thermometers?

to temperature. Even the slightest change in temprature can result in a
very noticiable difference in the hight of the mercury.
